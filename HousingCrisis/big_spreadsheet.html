Austin's Housing Crisis: 50 City Comparison

I am an economist studying the housing crisis in Austin, Texas. Part of my process is comparing Austin to other cities. I collected data from the 50 largest cities in America (by metro population) and compared them. Austin is in the middle of the pack — it is the 28th largest.

The data can be downloaded as a big spreadsheet.

High rent causes homelessness

It is pretty easy to see that high rent causes more of the population to be homeless.

  • Austin is the yellow square
  • Texas cities (Dallas, Houston, and San Antonio) are green triangles
  • California cities (Los Angeles, San Francisco, San Diego, San Jose, Riverside, and Sacramento) are large red circles
  • All other cities are small blue circles

It is also easy to see that high rent causes more homeless to go unsheltered

High rent is correlated with a high land price

Economists have traced the high cost of housing to cities' zoning laws. This is visible in the data as a high land price is correlated with high rent.

In the above graph, the "logarithm" function has been applied to the rents and land prices. It makes the cheaper priced lots visible along side the expensive ones. This is common practice in economics.

Other results

Some readers may care about these other results:
  • Cities with high rent reversed their growth during COVID. That is, their growth in 2021 was much lower than their growth over the last decade.
  • Growth of a city is no longer tied to its GDP. Economists would expect richer cities to grow, but something is stopping that process. The best explanation is zoning laws.

Download the data

The big spreadsheet includes:

  • Metro area population (US Census 2021 est., 2020, 2010)
  • City population (US Census 2021 est., 2020, 2010)
  • City's area
  • Price of a cheap lot in City (Zillow, 1%ile in Sept. 2021)
  • Price of a cheap apartment in City (1bed+1bath, Zillow 1%ile in Sept. 2021)
  • Median rent (HUD FY2023 and FY2013)
  • Metro GDP (2018, 2017)
  • Median lot size and price-per-sqft (from Angi.com)
  • Overall homeless and unsheltered homeless (HUD 2019)

Warning: Be aware that the data comes from regions with different boundaries. HUD's rents and homeless numbers are not consistently aligned with metro areas nor cities. The data has little correlation with the cities' areas, but ratios may matter.
